Definitions and Notation

Let $\alpha = ( \alpha_1, \alpha_2, \cdots, alpha_d) \in \mathbb{N}_0^d$ multiindex, and $G $

For a second-order partial differential equation, we denote the equation as follows:

$$ F(D^2 u, D^1 u, u, x) = -\Sigma^d_{i,j=0}a_{ij}(x) \cdot \partial_{x_i x_j}u + \Sigma^d_{i=0} b_i(x) \cdot \partial_{x_i}u + c(x)\cdot u - f(x) $$ Assume $A(x) := {a_{i,j}(x)} ^d _{i,j=0}$ is a symmetric matrix with eigenvalues $\lambda_0 {(x)} \leq \lambda_1 {(x)} \leq \cdots \leq \lambda_d {(x)}$. Now we can define 3 different types of PDE. Suppose $S={0,1,\cdots, d}$. At $x \in \mathbb R^{d+1}$, the PDE is called

  • elliptic $\iff$ $((\forall j \in S) (\lambda_j(x) \neq 0)) \and (\operatorname {sign}(\lambda_0(x)) = \operatorname {sign}(\lambda_1(x))=\cdots =\operatorname {sign}(\lambda_d(x)))$
  • parabolic $\iff$ $((\exist ! j \in S) (\lambda_j(x) = 0)) \and (\operatorname {rank}(A(x),B(x)) = d+1)$
  • hyperbolic $\iff$ $((\forall j \in S) (\lambda_j(x) \neq 0)) \and ((\exist! j \in S)((\forall k \in S /{j})(\operatorname {sign}(\lambda_j(x)) \neq \operatorname {sign}(\lambda_k(x))) $

The PDE is called elliptic/parabolic/hyperbolic on $G$, if it is elliptic/parabolic/hyperbolic $\forall x \in G$ .
